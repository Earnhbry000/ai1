How I Make EAS Alarms

So, I see you came here to find out how I make my EAS Alarms. These are something I do for fun so I will tell you how. Hope you like this Documentation.

Things You Will Need

You Will Need:
  • IMovie (In My Case) Or Any Video Editing Software
  • EAS Sounds(Avalible At Bottom Of Page)
  • Your EAS Message (Instructions On Next Step)
    • You Will Also Need These Websites:
  • Ttsdemo.com
  • PhotoMosh.com (Optional)
  • fontdrop.com

    • Step 1: Make Your Voice

    So, On this step you need to go to Ttsdemo.com. On that site do not look at the dog or person that is talking. Instead, Look to the Left Side. There is a voice selection option, select Tom (US).

    There Is a character limit of 600 on this site but you can bypass it by copying and pasting a script from a notepad or using the text box in the cite (will not bypass it). Please Make sure to use "," for a short pause, "." for a longer pause, and ",." for the longest pause in your script.

    After This, Use Anything from OBS Studio (for PC) or Apples Built-In Screen recorder For Apple, You can use a 3rd party screen recorder for Android.

    Step 2: Convert Video To Audio

    So, You HAve your Video recording. What you need to do is convert that into an audio file for easy placement. You can use:

  • Cloudconvert Video to Audio
  • Apple Shortcuts (Mobile)
    • Or You can use any way you want

    Step 3: Make A Eas Screen

    So on this 3rd step you need to make a screen for your Alarm. You cant have a black screen, right? I use fontdrop.com for making my alarm screen. Get The VCR-OSD-MONO file from the downloads at the bottom of this page. You need to drag and drop this file onto the website and Click "Type Yourself". Luckily, It already comes with a Black screen so it makes the design easier!

    Step 4: Editing

    So, This is most likely the last step to making this sort of thing, it is problably the hardest of them all though. Use the Sounds in the Bottom of the page to stitch the sounds and the picture together. And Now you have finished!
